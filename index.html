About
I am an Engineering DPhil student at St Peter's College, Oxford, where I am part of the Machine Learning Research Group, advised by Stephen Roberts.

I am excited by adaptive optimization algorithms, which can alter their behavior on the fly as learning progresses. Some manifestations of this interest are in reinforcement learning (RL), where we want a principled way to trade off exploration and exploitation, and in hyperparameter selection (which can be done online). I am also interested in compact neural networks, specifically for RL policies. Downstream, I am most interested in robotics and electronic trading.

Before coming to Oxford I was a VP in the Quantitative Research team at JPM in New York. While in America I studied for a Master's part-time at Columbia where I discovered the joys of machine learning research! I am originally from the UK, and studied Maths at Exeter.

I am immensely privileged to have some fantastic collaborators, namely Krzysztof Choromanski, Aldo Pacchiano and Yunhao Tang.
