About
I am an Engineering DPhil student at St Peter's College, Oxford, where I am part of the Machine Learning Research Group, advised by Stephen Roberts.

I am excited by adaptive algorithms, which can alter their behavior on the fly as learning progresses. Some manifestations of this interest are in reinforcement learning (RL), where we want a principled way to trade off exploration and exploitation, and in hyperparameter selection (which can be done online). To do this effectively, I am interested in finding a meaningful way to representing policy behaviors, such that we can use them to guide learning, for example by promoting diversity in the ''right'' dimensions. Downstream, I am most interested in real world autonomous agents in settings such as robotics and electronic trading.

Before coming to Oxford I was a VP in the Quantitative Research team at JPM in New York. While in America I studied for a Master's part-time at Columbia where I discovered the joys of machine learning research! I am originally from the UK, and studied Maths at Exeter.

I am immensely privileged to have worked with some fantastic people, in alphabetical order: Philip Ball, Krzysztof Choromanski, Aldo Pacchiano, Yunhao Tang.
